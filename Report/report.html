Cognitive Functioning:

The BNCE assesses the major cognitive functions usually targeted by neuropsychological testing, including disorders of memory, gnosis, praxis, language, orientation, attention, and executive functions. The items are simple and presented in two parts. All of the Part I subtests Orientation, Presidential Memory, Naming, Comprehension, and Constructive Praxis - are mainly related to more conventional types of information processing. These tasks are followed by a set of more complex tasks contained in the Part II subtests - Shifting Set, Incomplete Pictures, Similarities, Attention, and Working Memory. These subtests are aimed at the processing of novel, incomplete, and less conventional types of information - the kind of processing required for the successful performance of executive functions.
